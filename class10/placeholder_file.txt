There are many format specifiers we can use. Here are some common ones:

%b - boolean (true or false)
%d - decimal integer
%e - scientific notation with an exponent of e
%f - floating point
%g - the shorter of %e and %f
%o - octal (base 8)
%p - pointer (address)
%s - string
%i - Integer (base 10)
%u - Unsigned decimal (integer) number
%x - Hexadecimal number (base 16)
%t - Date/time
%c - character
%n - Newline
